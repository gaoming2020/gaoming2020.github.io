Why Tech CEOs Are Flocking to Trump: Kevin O'Leary Explains the Real Motivations

Posted on 2024-12-19

Why are some of the biggest names in tech, like the CEOs of Amazon, Apple, and Google, suddenly cozying up to Donald Trump? It’s not about personal fondness, says Kevin O’Leary, but rather cold, hard economics. In a recent interview, O’Leary didn’t mince words, declaring, “I’m licking my own chops right now.” So, what’s the real driving force behind this corporate migration to Mar-a-Lago?

First and foremost, O’Leary emphasizes the power of a clear mandate. He points out Trump’s strong position and the fact that ‘no one is going to touch him for two years’. This stability is highly attractive to businesses seeking predictable policy. This leads to the first key motivation: tax rates. Tech CEOs are likely breathing a sigh of relief knowing the 21% tax rate is likely to stay put, rather than the potential 28% they feared. This certainty allows for better financial planning and, let’s be honest, more profit.

Beyond taxes, O’Leary also highlighted the appeal of deregulation. These CEOs are also banking on significant deregulation and freedom of speech on social media – two factors that can dramatically impact their businesses. Less government intervention and a more ‘hands off’ approach to media is a significant draw for these companies. Think of it this way: these tech giants aren’t suddenly fans of Trump’s personality, they’re simply reacting to the tangible economic benefits they see on the horizon. O’Leary uses the term “sucking up” – highlighting the transactional nature of this relationship.

Finally, it’s worth noting O’Leary’s own bullish outlook on the situation. He believes that Trump is moving “to a new place” and is “delivering the mandate” – all things that point towards a more stable business climate. As he succinctly states, “These are good times”.

So, if you’re wondering why the tech elite are suddenly flocking to Mar-a-Lago, look beyond the headlines and focus on the money. Tax rates, deregulation, and a strong political mandate are the real drivers of this trend. What does this mean for the average person? That remains to be seen.
