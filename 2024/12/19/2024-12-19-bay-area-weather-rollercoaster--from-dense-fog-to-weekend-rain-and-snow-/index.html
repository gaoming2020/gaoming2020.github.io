Bay Area Weather Rollercoaster: From Dense Fog to Weekend Rain and Snow!

Posted on 2024-12-19

Is your morning commute feeling like a scene from a horror movie? You’re not alone! This week, the Bay Area is experiencing a wild weather ride, starting with some seriously stubborn fog. But don’t get too comfortable, because we’re about to transition into a weekend of rain, and yes, even some mountain snow!

Let’s break down what’s happening. First off, that fog. It’s been incredibly dense, especially inland and east of the Oakland hills, making visibility near zero. It’s not just annoying; it’s actually holding back temperatures! Once the fog clears in some areas, like San Jose and Los Gatos, we can expect to see temperatures reach the upper 60’s, even into the low 70’s, which is well above average for this time of year. If you’re in the fog zone, you’ll be seeing a couple days of cooler temperatures and limited sunshine.

Looking ahead, don’t get used to the mild afternoons. A new storm system is headed our way, bringing with it a series of rain chances starting Saturday. While it may not be a complete washout on Saturday, Sunday will have scattered showers. The heavier, more consistent rain will likely arrive Sunday night into Tuesday, so be prepared for some wet weather. The good news? It’s expected to be a series of waves, which should help prevent any major flooding. So, while the rain may be a bit inconvenient, it should be manageable.

And for those of you looking to hit the slopes, get excited! The mountains will start seeing some snow, with levels dropping from 8,000 to around 5,000 feet by Monday night and Tuesday. So, it might be time to dust off those winter tires. Remember to stay tuned to weather updates over the next week for the latest conditions. Get ready for fog, a few pleasant warm-ups, then rain, and snow on the horizon. Share this with your friends and let’s all brace ourselves for a wet and wild Christmas week!
