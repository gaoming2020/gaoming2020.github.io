Forget Mariah Carey: The Late Show Band's Holiday Commercials Are Here (And They're Wild!)

Posted on 2024-12-19

Okay, let’s be honest, the holiday season is synonymous with commercials. We’re bombarded with them from every angle, from the big-budget celebrity endorsements to the heartwarming, tear-jerker ads. But what happens when the incredibly talented house band of ‘The Late Show’ decides to throw their hats into the ring? Chaos, that’s what. And we’re absolutely here for it.

Forget the polished perfection of Mariah Carey for Kay Jewelers; the Late Show Band’s holiday commercial blitz is a hilarious breath of fresh air. It all started when Stephen Colbert jokingly wondered why they hadn’t capitalized on the season. Little did he know, they were more than ready to unleash their brand of quirky brilliance on the world.

First up, we have Joe Saylor with his ‘Silent Bells.’ Yes, you heard that right. Joe, recognizing the universal annoyance of jingle bells, created a sound-free version of all your holiday favorites, even a silent gong! Perfect for those seeking some much needed peace during the holidays or a distraction free fire alarm. He even has a slogan: ‘I have no ding dong.’ Brilliant!

Then, Endea Owens stepped up with a solution to the age-old problem of pea portion control. Her individually wrapped frozen peas aim to eliminate the ‘too many or too few’ dilemma, complete with a handy chart to calculate exactly how many peas to cook based on how many guests you have. Spoiler alert: it’s a LOT of peas. And don’t even get me started on the resealable bags for refreezing individual peas - a holiday miracle, or is it a cry for help?

And finally, Louis Cato himself revealed his masterpiece: Roof Wolves. Forget Santa and his clumsy reindeer ruining your shingles; Louis has you covered with wolf sculptures that howl and spray (yes, spray) authentic wolf urine. It’s the perfect solution to a reindeer problem you might not have known you had.

The Late Show Band has officially shown us that holiday commercials don’t have to be predictable. They can be weird, hilarious, and utterly unforgettable. So, while everyone else is watching tear-jerking ads, remember the unique products brought to you by the Late Show Band, and consider grabbing your silent bells, individually wrapped peas and roof wolves today! Be sure to share your thoughts on these unorthodox ads on social media!
