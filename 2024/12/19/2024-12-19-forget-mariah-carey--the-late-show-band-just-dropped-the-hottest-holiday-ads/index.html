Forget Mariah Carey! The Late Show Band Just Dropped the Hottest Holiday Ads

Posted on 2024-12-19

Move over, Snoop Dogg and Charli XCX, there’s a new crew taking over the holiday commercial game, and they’re not who you’d expect! You know ‘The Late Show’ band, the incredibly talented musicians who provide the soundtrack to Stephen Colbert’s antics? Well, they just unveiled their very own, shall we say… unique… holiday ad campaign, and it’s pure comedic gold.

The whole thing kicked off with Stephen lamenting the abundance of celebrity endorsements this time of year. Then, boom, it’s revealed that Joe Saylor, the band’s percussionist, had been holding out on us with his silent bells! Yes, you heard that right, silent bells. He explains it’s for people who are annoyed by the constant jingle and even includes a silent fire alarm. His slogan? “I have no ding dong.” Brilliant, right?

But the ad madness doesn’t stop there. Endea Owens enters the fray with a solution for every holiday dinner host: Individually wrapped frozen peas! She acknowledges the universal fear of having too many or too few peas. Endea’s chart helps you find the right serving size, whether you need 819 peas for seven guests or extra for surprise visitors. And you can re-freeze them. It’s utterly ridiculous, yet totally genius.

Finally, we have Louis Cato, who is not about to be outdone. He presents…Roof Wolves. Forget string lights; protect your roof from Santa and his clumsy reindeer with these howling, urine-spraying sculptures! The visuals and the concept alone are enough to make anyone laugh. Seriously, could you imagine these on your roof? The tagline: “Protect What Matters Most.” And, in this case, that means your shingles.

So what’s the takeaway from all this? Holiday advertising doesn’t have to be the same old schmaltz. ‘The Late Show’ band proved you can be creative, bizarre, and laugh-out-loud funny, and that’s exactly what makes these commercials so unforgettable. Forget the traditional ads; give us more silent bells, individually wrapped peas, and roof-defending wolves. What are your thoughts? Let us know in the comments and share this ridiculousness with your friends!
