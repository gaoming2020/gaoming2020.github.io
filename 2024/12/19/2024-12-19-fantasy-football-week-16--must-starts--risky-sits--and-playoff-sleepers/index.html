Fantasy Football Week 16: Must-Starts, Risky Sits, and Playoff Sleepers

Posted on 2024-12-19

The fantasy football playoffs are here, and one wrong move can send your season spiraling. If you’re sweating over your Week 16 lineup, you’re not alone! This week is all about making the right calls to get you into that championship game.

Quarterback Quandaries: Who to Ride and Who to Bench

The QB landscape is volatile, with injuries and inconsistent performances making things tricky. One player you shouldn’t bench? Jayden Daniels. Despite a disappointing previous outing against the Eagles, he’s been a fantasy machine, averaging 27 points per game since then. The Eagles defense has actually allowed some solid QB performances, making Daniels a must-start with his rushing upside.

Another QB to consider is Bo Nix, who’s been on fire lately. Multiple passing touchdowns in four of his last five, and he’s facing a struggling Chargers defense that’s allowing tons of yards and touchdowns. Like Daniels, Nix offers dual-threat value, crucial for late-season fantasy success. Conversely, Brock Purdy is a firm sit this week. His recent form has been abysmal, with minimal fantasy points and a low TD rate. The Dolphins’ stingy defense against QBs further cements his bench status.

Running Backs: The James Connor Show and Some Risky Business

If you drafted or traded for James Connor, now is the time to rejoice! He was RB1 last week and faces the Panthers, who allow the most rushing yards and touchdowns to running backs in the league. This is a recipe for a dominant performance and potential league-winning production. On the other end of the spectrum, Isaiah Pacheco is looking shaky after his return. He’s splitting carries with Kareem Hunt and his explosiveness isn’t quite there. With the Chiefs O-line struggles and a tough matchup against the Texans, bench Pacheco this week. Najee Harris is another RB to steer clear of. His production has drastically declined with Jalen Warren’s return and the Ravens’ league-best run defense poses a significant challenge.

Sleeper Alert: A Dash of Cooper Rush and Rhamondre Stevenson

Looking for some under-the-radar players to fill out your lineup? Cooper Rush has been quietly putting up numbers, racking up 8 touchdowns in the last four games. He’s facing the Buccaneers, who allow the second-most fantasy points to QBs. At running back, keep an eye on Rhamondre Stevenson. He might see an uptick in usage due to his matchup against the Bills, who are vulnerable against RBs. And don’t forget Mac Jones, who has quietly been a top-10 QB in two of his last 3 games, facing a porous Raiders secondary.

Wide Receiver and Tight End Must-Starts and Sits: The Curious Case of Jonnu Smith

Jason should be in your lineup. He’s been on fire, averaging 20 fantasy points per game, which is WR2 levels. Another receiver to consider is Ladd McConkey, who is showing real consistency with the slot snaps he is receiving and facing the 5th most Man coverage team. On the flip side, Amari Cooper’s volatility is alarming. He can’t be trusted after a recent zero-catch performance. At tight end, the rise of Jonnu Smith is truly fascinating. He’s closer to TE2 than TE6, averaging over 20 fantasy points per game in his last 5. Despite a tough matchup, he’s simply too good to sit. However, Travis Kelce is a risky sit this week with a noticeable dip in production. He’s not the dominant force of previous years. Consider options such as Brenton Strange, who saw a massive increase in volume last week. Another potential sleeper is Keenan Allen, who has been on a tear recently, and also consider Calvin Austin who has been getting the opportunities in the slot, against a vulnerable defense.

Week 16 is critical, so don’t let bad decisions ruin your playoff run. Do your research, trust your gut, and get ready for some high-stakes fantasy football!
