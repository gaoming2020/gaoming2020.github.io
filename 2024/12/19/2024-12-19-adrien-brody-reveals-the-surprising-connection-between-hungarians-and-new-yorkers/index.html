Adrien Brody Reveals the Surprising Connection Between Hungarians and New Yorkers

Posted on 2024-12-19

Ever wondered what links the passionate energy of Hungarians with the outspoken nature of New Yorkers? Turns out, there’s more in common than you might think! In a recent interview, actor Adrien Brody, known for his roles in films like “The Pianist” and “The Grand Budapest Hotel,” shared some fascinating insights into his Hungarian heritage and how it relates to his latest film, “The Brutalist.”

Brody’s connection to Hungary is deeply personal; his mother is a Hungarian immigrant who fled the country during the 1956 revolution. This experience, he explains, is why playing the role of a Hungarian architect in “The Brutalist” resonates so strongly with him. He not only channeled his grandfather’s pronounced Hungarian accent but also tapped into the unique “Hungarian sensibility” he grew up with. This included the passion and enthusiasm that he experienced growing up as a child.

But what exactly is that sensibility? According to Brody, Hungarians, much like New Yorkers, are wonderfully enthusiastic, passionate, and somewhat outspoken. He notes that their humor often has a dark edge, where a joke might be laden with truth. It is an interesting way to describe the unique personalities that are often a staple of the city of New York. This frankness and intensity, Brody suggests, might be initially startling if you’re unfamiliar with it, but ultimately it’s a testament to their authenticity. It’s a cultural similarity that is not often discussed, but the similarities are quite interesting.

“The Brutalist” explores themes of immigration, sacrifice, and the search for identity, all topics close to Brody’s heart. He also shares a clip from the film featuring him and Guy Pearce in a powerful scene. In it, his character, Laszlo Toth, is moved by the discovery that his architectural creations in Europe had survived the Nazi occupation. It is a moment of reflection that showcases the importance of history and remembrance.

Adrien Brody’s personal story not only adds depth to his acting career but also sheds light on the complex identities that define our global community. Whether it’s the bustling streets of New York or the heart of Budapest, the human experience is wonderfully diverse, and this film looks to explore the immigrant experience. Intrigued?
