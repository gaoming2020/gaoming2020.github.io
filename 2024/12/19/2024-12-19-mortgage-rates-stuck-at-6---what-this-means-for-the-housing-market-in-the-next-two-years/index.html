Mortgage Rates Stuck at 6%? What This Means for the Housing Market in the Next Two Years

Posted on 2024-12-19

Are you trying to navigate the confusing world of real estate? You’re not alone. Recent predictions from Compass CEO Robert Reffkin suggest we might be seeing mortgage rates hovering around 6% for the next two years. But what does this really mean for you, whether you’re a buyer, seller, or just keeping an eye on the market?

The Mortgage Rate Outlook

Forget the hopes of plummeting interest rates anytime soon. The expectation of mortgage rates dropping into the 5% range is off the table, at least for the next couple of years. According to Reffkin, the forecast is for a stable rate of around 6%. This is significant because it impacts affordability and overall buyer confidence. While this might not be the news some were hoping for, understanding this stability is crucial for making informed decisions.

Inventory is Key (and Still Low)

One of the biggest factors influencing the housing market is inventory. While there has been a year-over-year increase in available homes, we’re still about 20% below pre-pandemic levels. This shortage particularly affects the North, Northeast, and Midwest regions. Interestingly, Compass is experimenting with creating more “private exclusive” listings – a strategy they’ve borrowed from builders. By not listing homes on all third-party sites, they aim to minimize negative perceptions, thus encouraging more people to list. While this is helping to create inventory in some areas, we still need to see a larger increase across the board to bring prices down or stabilize them further.

What to Expect in Prices & Should You Rent or Buy?

With mortgage rates expected to remain stable and a modest increase in home prices (3-5% next year, and potentially another 5% the following year), the market is looking relatively stable – just not booming. For those asking the age-old question, ‘Is it better to rent or buy right now?’ The answer might surprise you. According to the data presented in the video, buying seems to be the more economical option. While prices have remained pretty flat over the past two years, rental costs in certain areas like Manhattan have increased by a whopping 36% over the past three years! So, if you’re in a place to buy, the market might be more favorable than you think.

The Takeaway

The real estate market is always changing and while this data is informative, it’s not set in stone. It’s crucial to stay informed and consider all your options. So, whether you’re buying, selling or just interested, it’s important to understand current trends. Are you ready to explore your options?
