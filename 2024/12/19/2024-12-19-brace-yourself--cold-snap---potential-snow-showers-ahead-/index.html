Brace Yourself: Cold Snap & Potential Snow Showers Ahead!

Posted on 2024-12-19

Feeling that chill in the air? You’re not alone! It seems like Mother Nature is finally remembering it’s December and sending a dose of colder weather our way. But before you start hiding under the blankets, let’s break down what to expect over the next few days.

A Calm Before the Cold

The good news? We’re not jumping straight into an arctic blast. Today and tomorrow are actually pretty decent for this time of year. Expect temperatures around the low 40s, which is pretty much average for late December. You might even catch a bit of sunshine peeking through the clouds. Perfect for finishing up those last-minute holiday errands without freezing your fingers off. So if you need to brave the stores, now’s your chance! It looks like Thursday will be pretty calm and mostly dry.

The Cold Front Arrives

Friday is when things get a bit more interesting. A cold front is moving in, bringing with it the chance of snow flurries and snow showers. The temperatures will also take a tumble, dropping down to the mid-30s. While we are unlikely to see any significant accumulation, be prepared for potentially wet roads in the morning. It’s not a blizzard, but it’s definitely a reminder that winter is here. Consider Friday a ‘Weather Impact Day’ – take extra care while traveling. Bundle up and maybe grab an extra cup of something warm before heading out.

Looking Ahead: A Warmer Christmas?

While the weekend will be chilly with highs near freezing and overnight lows in the teens and twenties, things are about to get warmer. Just in time for the holidays, it looks like we’ll be experiencing a significant warm-up. Temperatures will climb back into the 40s, possibly even near 50. The trade off will be some wet weather and a grey feel similar to today, so if you were hoping for a white Christmas, that doesn’t look likely. Instead, expect a warmer, albeit potentially damp, holiday season.

Key Takeaway: Stay informed and prepared for the upcoming weather changes. Friday will be the coldest day of the week with the chance of snow. Stay safe, and enjoy the last moments of the holiday season!
