Inside Simone Giertz's Cozy 630 sqft LA Home: A Masterclass in Custom Living

Posted on 2024-12-19

Ever dreamed of a home that feels like a warm hug? That’s exactly what inventor Simone Giertz has created in her 630 square foot Los Angeles abode. Forget cookie-cutter designs; Simone’s space is a testament to personal expression, clever problem-solving, and a healthy dose of ‘weirdness’. From a fire alarm turned key holder to a plant-covered lamp (dubbed ‘Plamp’), her home is a delightful showcase of what happens when creativity meets necessity.

Small Space, Big Ideas

Simone’s home isn’t just about fitting into a small space; it’s about making that space work for her. She’s taken the constraints of a small, one-bedroom house and turned them into design prompts, transforming everyday objects into custom solutions. Take, for example, the staircase to the loft. Instead of just stairs, she’s built in shoe storage and jacket cabinets, addressing a major architectural pet peeve: the lack of entry hallways in American homes. This isn’t just about saving space; it’s about thoughtful design that enhances daily life. This custom approach resonates with her belief that ‘everything is custom’ rather than just ‘everything is awesome’.

Nods to Heritage & Humor

While her home is full of modern ingenuity, Simone doesn’t shy away from her roots. She includes a family bridal chest that dates back to 1733, a beautiful link to her Swedish heritage. There is a unique blend of functional innovation and nostalgic design. A thread spool side table that actually stores yarn and even a mechanical fruit bowl that expands or contracts depending on the contents, shows that her playful personality shines through every detail. Even a commissioned oil painting depicting her dramatic pose while building a table adds humor to the space.

A Living Canvas

More than just a dwelling, Simone sees her house as a constant experiment, a creative canvas where nothing is truly permanent. From custom light switches to her ‘coat hinger’ design (a foldable coat hanger inspired by her lack of closet space), her home embodies the idea of ‘weird over boring’. She embraces the idea that you can always revert to the boring version if your experiments don’t pan out. The laundry chute is also an escape hatch for small humans and possibly the dog.

Simone Giertz’s home is more than just four walls; it’s a reflection of her mind. It challenges the notion that small spaces limit creativity, showing us that with a bit of ingenuity and a lot of personal flair, you can transform any space into a truly extraordinary home. Ready to inject some personality into your own living space? Start small, get creative, and don’t be afraid to embrace the weird!
