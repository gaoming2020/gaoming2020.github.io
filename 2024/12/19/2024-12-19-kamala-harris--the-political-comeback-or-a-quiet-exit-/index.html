Kamala Harris: The Political Comeback or a Quiet Exit?

Posted on 2024-12-19

Is Kamala Harris quietly fading away, or is she gearing up for a political resurgence? It’s a question that’s been swirling since her less-than-stellar performance as a presidential nominee and Vice President. You’d think she might be out of the spotlight, but sources suggest she’s eyeing a run for California Governor in 2026 or even another shot at the presidency in 2028. But is this a realistic ambition, or wishful thinking?

Some commentators point to her recent actions, or lack thereof, suggesting a form of ‘quiet quitting’ from the political scene. After a disappointing election cycle, it seems she retreated for a period. This could signal either a temporary regrouping or a more permanent departure from the national stage. Is she burnt out, or just strategizing her next move? The political landscape is littered with politicians who have bounced back from setbacks, so a complete write-off of Harris would be premature.

However, the criticism is harsh. Many argue that she lacks a clear platform and failed to resonate with key demographics, including youth and African-American voters. Some suggest that she’s a symbol of broken promises and a reminder of the Biden administration’s perceived failures. She was propped up by media, they say, but couldn’t deliver. The question arises: What can Kamala run on? Her time as VP was marked by issues like the border crisis, inflation and the fentanyl crisis. All this has fueled doubts whether she can rally support for a future campaign.

The truth is, Kamala Harris’s future remains uncertain. She could embrace the Al Gore model, moving into a different arena. She may be trying to figure out what she did wrong, and what her purpose in politics is. It’s crucial to understand her recent failures to formulate a viable path forward. Ultimately, the voters will decide. But one thing is clear: this isn’t the last we’ve heard of Kamala Harris. Whether it’s a comeback or a quiet exit, her story is far from over. What do you think?
