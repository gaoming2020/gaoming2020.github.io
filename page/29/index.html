The real magic happens when you start looking at this in combination with other patterns. Here’s the lowdown:

  • Spotting the Setup: The first thing you need to do is look at the one-hour timeframe for an existing high timeframe Fair Value Gap. A clear gap in price action on the chart is the first sign.   • The Liquidity Grab and FVG Combo: After identifying the higher timeframe gap, drop down to a lower timeframe, the video recommends the five-minute chart, for your entry. We are now looking for very specific signs. First is a grab of liquidity to the downside. Next, we look at the the price action that has occurred around our liquidity grab. Look for a bearish fair value gap, then observe if a bullish fair value gap was created in this area. These are all bullish signs. This formation is key, and its reliability makes it a valuable strategy for traders. You wait for the price to come down and enter this fair value gap.

  • Profit and Risk Management: Now, where should you take profit? Using your Fibonacci retracement tool you should mark from the liquidity grab to the recent high. Your take profit zone should be at the standard deviation of 2. And your stop loss should be placed just below the recent low.
